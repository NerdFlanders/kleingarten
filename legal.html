Terms & Conditions

Last updated: March 1, 2024

Allotment Plot Rules

  • Plot Maintenance: All plot holders must maintain their assigned area in good condition and keep it free from weeds that may spread to neighboring plots. Regular cultivation and upkeep are required.
  • Organic Practices: Only organic gardening methods are permitted. No chemical pesticides, herbicides, or synthetic fertilizers may be used. We promote sustainable and environmentally friendly practices.
  • Water Usage: Water is provided for garden use only. Please use responsibly and report any leaks immediately. Water conservation is everyone's responsibility.
  • Structures: Small garden structures such as trellises, cold frames, and small sheds are permitted with prior approval from the garden committee. All structures must be safe and well-maintained.

Membership Terms

  • Annual Fees: Plot fees are due by March 1st each year. Late payments may result in plot reassignment. Payment plans are available for those who need them.
  • Community Service: All members are expected to contribute 8 hours of community service annually through garden maintenance, events, or committee work. This helps maintain our community spirit.
  • Plot Assignment: Plots are assigned on a first-come, first-served basis. Waiting list members will be contacted when plots become available. Priority may be given to returning members.
  • Termination: Membership may be terminated for violation of garden rules, non-payment of fees, or behavior that disrupts the community harmony.

Safety & Liability

  • Garden Hours: The garden is open from dawn to dusk, 7 days a week. Access outside these hours is prohibited for safety and security reasons.
  • Children: Children must be supervised at all times while in the garden area. Parents are responsible for their children's safety and behavior.
  • Tools & Equipment: Members are responsible for their own tools and equipment. Shared tools must be returned clean and in good condition. Report any damaged equipment immediately.
  • Liability: Members participate at their own risk. Green Haven Allotments is not liable for injuries, theft, or property damage. We recommend members have appropriate insurance coverage.

Community Guidelines

  • Respect: Treat all members, their property, and the shared garden space with respect. We are a diverse community united by our love of gardening.
  • Sharing: Members are encouraged to share knowledge, surplus produce, and resources with the community. This spirit of sharing makes our garden special.
  • Noise: Keep noise levels reasonable, especially during early morning and evening hours. Be considerate of neighbors and nearby residents.
  • Parking: Use designated parking areas only and do not block access roads or neighboring properties. Carpooling is encouraged when possible.

Environmental Responsibility

  • Composting: All organic waste must be properly composted in designated areas. We provide community compost bins for this purpose.
  • Waste Management: All non-organic waste must be removed from the garden. Recycling is encouraged wherever possible.
  • Native Plants: Priority should be given to native and drought-resistant plants that support local wildlife and require less water.
  • Chemical-Free Zone: Our garden is a chemical-free environment. This protects our soil, water, and the health of all community members.

Dispute Resolution

Any disputes between members or concerns about garden operations should be addressed through the following process:

  1. Attempt to resolve the issue directly with the involved parties in a respectful and constructive manner
  2. If unresolved, bring the matter to the Garden Committee for mediation within 30 days of the initial concern
  3. The Committee will investigate and mediate, making a binding decision within two weeks
  4. Appeals may be made to the full membership at the next scheduled annual meeting

All disputes will be handled confidentially and with respect for all parties involved.

Seasonal Guidelines

  • Spring Preparation: All plots must be prepared for planting by April 1st. This includes clearing winter debris and soil preparation.
  • Summer Maintenance: Regular watering, weeding, and harvesting are required during growing season. Plots showing neglect may be reassigned.
  • Fall Cleanup: All plots must be cleaned and prepared for winter by November 15th. Remove all plant debris and secure any structures.
  • Winter Access: Limited access during winter months for maintenance and planning. Check with committee for specific winter guidelines.
